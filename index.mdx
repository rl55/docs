**iduScore** (from [Idunox](https://iduscore.com)) helps people understand **wellbeing**—how well they are likely to feel and function day to day over the coming years—using **routine blood markers** and a **short lifestyle questionnaire**. Reports summarize **four areas** (cognitive, cardiovascular, respiratory, and renal) and a **long-horizon outlook** (on the order of 8–10 years), using machine-learning models trained on large research datasets such as the [UK Biobank](https://www.ukbiobank.ac.uk/).

As a **B2B partner**, you collect the agreed markers and answers, then use this site to implement the **partner JSON API**: authenticate, submit de-identified payloads, track jobs, receive **webhooks**, and retrieve **wellbeing-focused** results for your end users.

## Partner API at a glance

The API is a **partner-facing REST contract** for **Type A** (canonical JSON) submissions—not FHIR at ingestion. Typical flows:

1. **Authenticate** every `/v1/*` request with `X-Api-Key` or `Authorization: Bearer` (see OpenAPI for details).
2. **Submit** de-identified lab markers and/or report metadata; include non-empty **`x-correlation-id`** and **`Idempotency-Key`** on writes (`POST`).
3. **Poll** submission and job endpoints, or implement **HTTPS webhooks** you register with the platform.
4. **Fetch results** using the read APIs; responses use **IduScore wellbeing** language (stable outcome ids, plain-English labels, and optional notices).

All resource identifiers are **tenant-scoped** to the credentials you use.

## Product context (partner api integration)

For how end users experience IduScore—report contents, science overview, and FAQs—see [iduscore.com](https://iduscore.com). Partner documentation here focuses on **contracts, headers, payloads, and operational behavior** of the API.

<Note>
  **Regulatory framing:** IduScore is **not a medical device** and is for **information and education**, not diagnosis or treatment decisions. Partners must present outputs appropriately to end users and follow applicable law and clinical guidance in their jurisdictions. See the [iduScore disclaimer](https://iduscore.com) on the public site for the full consumer-facing language.
</Note>
